# Heuristics and Rules of Thumb

Entrepreneurs rely on these pithy rules of thumb to cut through noise, maintain momentum, and force immediate action.

## NOW, not how
Take immediate action rather than getting bogged down in the logistics of execution. Stop thinking so much and begin before you are ready.
> "NOW, not how."


## Do the Service First
If you want to start a SaaS business, do the service manually before you make the software.
> "If you want to start a SaaS business, just do the service before you make the software and then create software to do that service."


## Double Down on Growth
Do more of what makes you grow; maximize what is already working before moving on to a new project.
> "Do more of what makes you grow."


## State Your Ask and Shut Up
In negotiations, state your number or your request clearly, and then remain completely silent. Let the other person respond.
> "You say your ask or your number and you shut up."


## Zero Profit Target
Aim to have $0 dollars in profit at the end of every year by reinvesting it all back into the business to compound growth.
> "Aim to have $0 dollars in profit at the end of every year."


## Ask "Does it help us grow?"
Before pursuing any new idea, ask if it directly contributes to your primary growth goal. If not, discard it.


## No ask, no get
You must actively put yourself out there and ask for what you want. People cannot read your mind.
> "No ask, no get."


## Annoyances are opportunities
All of your own problems, annoyances, and inconveniences are literal business opportunities waiting to be solved.


## Buy the Book
If there's ever a book you're thinking about, just buy it immediately. The cost is negligible compared to the potential ROI.
> "If there’s ever a book you’re thinking about, just get it."


## Work for free until busy
Do work for free to build momentum and referrals until your schedule is full, then start charging.
> "Do it for free until you're too busy."


## Ask for Referrals from Rejections
Whenever you get rejected in sales or recruiting, always ask the person for a referral.
> "Always ask for referrals from rejections."


## Creativity replaces capital
If you don't have money, use creativity to structure deals, get assets, or market your product.
> "If you don't have money, creativity is your friend."


## Fail quick, iterate fast
Test ideas rapidly with minimal investment to learn and adapt quickly.
> "Fail quick / iterate fast."
